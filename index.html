Negative Effects of Fentanyl on young adults

Introduction
Drug addiction is a global public health issue that is getting worse. Illegal drug usage has killed many people and had other severe effects on people, especially young adults. Fentanyl, a synthetic opioid that is more potent than other opioids like morphine and heroin, is one of the illegal substances that have just recently come to be recognized as a serious hazard to public health.

A significant percentage of overdose deaths worldwide are attributable to fentanyl, and individuals who misuse it suffer very damaging effects on their physical and mental health. The purpose of this thesis website is to examine how fentanyl affects young adults in comparison to other illegal substances.

DEA
A sizable percentage of opioid overdose fatalities in recent years have been caused by the substance fentanyl. When the drug started to be combined with other narcotics like heroin and cocaine in the 2010s, it first attracted a lot of attention.The drug has been linked to a considerable number of opioid overdose deaths in recent years, according to the research, which emphasizes the effect fentanyl has had on the opioid epidemic in the United States.

The majority of the fentanyl that enters the United States, according to a report by the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA), is produced in China before being smuggled into the nation using a variety of methods, including shipping and mail services.

What's being done?
  • Disrupting the supply chain and expanding addiction treatment and prevention initiatives have been the main goals of attempts to stop the flow of fentanyl into the country
  • Despite these initiatives, fentanyl continues to pose a serious problem for law enforcement and public health professionals.
What is it?
Fentanyl is an extremely potent synthetic painkiller that has 50–100 times the potency of morphine. It was first created in the 1960s to be used as a surgical anesthetic, but it is now frequently used to treat chronic pain and to manage pain in cancer patients. Transdermal patches, lozenges, or injections containing fentanyl are frequently recommended.

Given its high potential for abuse and overdose, fentanyl has, unfortunately, become a real concern. The popularity of illicitly generated fentanyl in drug combinations with heroin and cocaine has increased, which has resulted in a spike in overdose deaths. Another delivery method for fentanyl is in the form of pills and powder, which are frequently sold on the black market alone or in combination with other prescription medications.

Social Media
  • Apps like Snapchat and TikTok have made it easy for youth to access drugs.
  • Around 90% of pills bought through social media are laced with fentanyl.
  • 4 out of 10 pills contain lethal doses of fentanyl, according to the DEA.
  • Parents and young adults need to understand the dangers of getting drugs through social media.
Society and Youth
  • The opioid epidemic is a result of overprescription of legal pain medications and foreign drug cartels trafficking fentanyl.
  • Fentanyl has caused health problems such as high rates of hepatitis C, HIV, and an 80% increase in newborns exposed to drugs.
  • The opioid epidemic has led to an increase in children in foster care, negatively impacting the economy and posing a national security issue.
  • Opioid misuse and overdoses cost the US about $1 trillion in 2017 and contribute to the decline of labor force participation amongst “prime age workers” by 20% in men and 25% in women.
Urgency
  • The opioid epidemic poses a serious threat to public health and safety.
  • It is crucial to address the overprescription of legal pain medications and to address the foreign drug cartels trafficking fentanyl.
  • Parents and young adults need to be educated on the dangers of opioids and the importance of seeking help if necessary.
  • We need to work together to combat the opioid epidemic to protect the well-being of our society and youth.

Overdose Symptoms:
  • Slow, weak, or no breathing
  • Drowsiness / Dizziness
  • Nausea / Vomiting
  • Pinpoint pupils
  • Disorientation
  • Choking or gurgling
  • Limp body
  • Discolored skin (lips and nails)
  • Cold and clammy skin

Negative Effects
  • CNS, gastrointestinal, cardiovascular, and pulmonary systems affected
  • Digestive symptoms like nausea, vomiting, constipation
  • Neurological symptoms like migraines, dizziness, vertigo, confusion, hallucinations
Long-term
  • Minor brain injuries can become severe with concussions and hypoxia
  • Respiratory depression and cardiovascular effects can have a negative toll
  • Chronic brain damage associated with physical, mental, and cognitive disabilities
Risks
  • Up to 100 times more potent than morphine
  • Highly addictive and potentially fatal even in modest dosages
  • Frequently combined with other narcotics, increasing risk of overdose
  • Can potentially result in coma, death, and respiratory depression
  • Support is crucial for those with addiction problems.

Community First Responder

It is important to understand the signs of an overdose and when it is appropriate to use Narcan. Narcan should be given to an individual that shows signs of an opioid overdose or when it is expected. The only FDA approved methods of using it for overdoses are using it as nasal spray or injecting it into the muscles, under the skin, or into the veins. Naloxone only works in the body for 30 to 90 minutes, so keep in mind that this is a quick remedy to an overdose. Overdoses have to be treated properly after narcan use, as it's possible for a person to still experience the effects of an overdose. People who are given naloxone need to be monitored another two hours after their last dose to make sure their breathing does not slow or stop.

More Info
Fentanyl addiction treatments can include medications and counseling. Medications for opioid use disorders are effective and save lives. Methadone and Buprenorphine both attach to and activate opioid receptors to ease the withdrawal symptoms and craving. Behavioral therapies like cognitive behavioral therapy, helps people to modify their attitudes and behaviors towards drug use, increase healthy life skills and a healthy lifestyle, and will help these individuals that are struggling with their medication. Cognitive behavioral therapy helps people recovering to manage their triggers and stress.

Methods
  • Methadone
